This Is the Real Root Cause of Knee Pain

It has nothing to do with your age, weight, or “wear and tear”.

The real root cause of knee pain is misalignment of the knee joint.

Check out the image below:

This part of your knee is called the patella (or kneecap).

It’s like a screw that holds your knee together.

When it’s “screwed” tightly, the patella ensures that the knee moves efficiently and without unnecessary strain on the joint.

It helps distribute the forces exerted on the knee joint more evenly.

This reduces the stress on the underlying bones and cartilage.

Now, here’s the problem…

Over the years, your patella can get loose and out of place.

It stops holding the knee together; instead, the entire joint gets misaligned, leading to uneven weight and pressure distribution.

Now, with every step, you are putting strain directly on cartilage.

This ultimately leads to osteoarthritis.

Let me say that again:

Every Step You Make, You Cartilage Gets More Damaged

With your patella out of place, every step you make puts tremendous pressure on your cartilage.
